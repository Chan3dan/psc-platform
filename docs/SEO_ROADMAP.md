# Niyukta SEO Roadmap

This roadmap is built for `https://niyukta.vercel.app`, with a Nepal-first focus on Loksewa preparation and the exam tracks most relevant to the product:

- `Computer Operator`
- `NaSu`
- `Kharidar`
- `Loksewa general preparation`

It is designed to work alongside [SEO_SETUP_GUIDE.md](./SEO_SETUP_GUIDE.md).

## 1. Goals

Primary goals:

- Rank for high-intent Loksewa exam queries in Nepal
- Become the strongest `Computer Operator` preparation hub first
- Expand authority into `NaSu` and `Kharidar`
- Capture both Nepali and English mixed search behavior
- Turn search traffic into repeat users for notes, MCQs, mock tests, and planner usage

Success metrics:

- Top 3 rankings for `computer operator syllabus nepal`, `computer operator mock test nepal`, and related variants
- Growth in Search Console impressions and clicks for Loksewa queries
- Increased visits to exam pages, notes pages, and mock test pages
- More returning users from daily question and weekly mock content

## 7. Internal Linking Model

Use a hub-and-spoke structure.

### From exam hub pages link to:

- syllabus
- notes
- mock tests
- model questions
- subject pages
- practical guide

### From subject pages link to:

- related notes
- related MCQ practice
- full mock test
- old questions
- exam hub page

### From blog posts link to:

- the exact exam hub
- one relevant notes page
- one relevant mock page
- one related FAQ or subject page

### Good anchor examples

- `computer operator syllabus`
- `na su old questions`
- `kharidar model question`
- `loksewa public management MCQ`

Avoid:

- `click here`
- `read more`
- `this page`

## 9. Technical SEO Priorities

### Highest priority

- Improve homepage and exam-page LCP
- Use `next/image` for major visible assets
- Keep important public pages indexable without login
- Ensure canonical tags exist for public landing pages
- Expand sitemap with exam, notes, and blog pages
- Avoid indexable thin pages with no content

### Current focus areas for Niyukta

- Keep homepage, exam pillars, notes pages, and syllabus pages lightweight
- Make mobile layout stable, especially feed and modal-heavy screens
- Keep PDFs and notes pages crawl-safe with descriptive landing text around them
- Add breadcrumb schema to exam and subject pages

### Schema to implement

- `Organization`
- `WebSite`
- `BreadcrumbList`
- `FAQPage`
- `Article`
- `Course`

### Crawlability checklist

- `robots.txt` allows public content
- `sitemap.xml` includes all public exam pages
- no accidental `noindex` on homepage or exam hubs
- no JS-only content for critical public landing pages
